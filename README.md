# Translayer

A tool that reads your engineering work and turns it into plain English
for the people who depend on it but don't speak code.

---

## The Problem

Engineers and business teams work on the same product but live in completely
different worlds.

A data migration finishes. The engineer knows it took 3 weeks, touched 80M
rows, had two near-misses, and involved decisions that will affect the system
for years. The product manager knows: "it's done."

That gap isn't just frustrating — it causes bad decisions, misaligned
priorities, and a slow breakdown of trust between the people building and the
people depending on what's built.

I've lived this firsthand. I own a full production data migration end-to-end
at my company — from the code to the business conversations. The translation
work is manual, exhausting, and shouldn't be.

Translayer automates it.

---

## What It Does

Point Translayer at your git history, migration logs, or PR descriptions and
it generates a plain-English summary — the kind you'd give in a 2-minute
standup if your PM actually wanted to understand what happened.

Not documentation. Not release notes. Human-readable context, written for
the person who needs to make decisions based on your work but can't read
your code.
